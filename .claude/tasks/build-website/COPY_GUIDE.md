# Copy Guide

## Voice

Use language that is:

- clear
- direct
- professional
- simple
- trustworthy
- practical

Avoid:

- hype
- vague buzzwords
- inflated promises
- fake urgency
- medical advice
- legal overclaims

## Core headline options

Preferred:

```text
Recover missed patient calls with automatic SMS follow-up
```

Alternative:

```text
Give missed callers a simple way back to your front desk
```

Alternative:

```text
Turn missed dental calls into follow-up conversations
```

## Subheadline direction

Use this direction:

```text
When your dental office misses a call, the caller gets a professional text so your team can reconnect.
```

## Short product explanation

Use:

```text
Missed Calls Dental helps dental offices follow up after missed calls with a short, professional SMS.
```

## How it works copy

Use short step labels:

```text
A call is missed
The caller gets a text
The patient replies
Your team follows up
```

Supporting text:

```text
A patient calls your office and the call is missed, busy, or unanswered.
A professional SMS is sent using your clinic identity.
The caller can reply directly.
Your front desk reviews replies and continues the conversation.
```

## SMS example

Safe example:

```text
Hi, this is Bright Smile Dental. We missed your call. Would you like help scheduling an appointment?
```

Alternative:

```text
Thanks for reaching out to Bright Smile Dental. We missed your call, but we can still help. Reply here and our team will follow up.
```

## Benefits

Use practical benefits:

### Respond faster

```text
Send a prompt follow-up after a missed call.
```

### Recover more callers

```text
Give missed callers an easy way back to your office.
```

### Reduce manual follow-up

```text
Help the front desk focus on replies that need attention.
```

### Keep communication professional

```text
Use short, clear texts that identify the clinic and support opt-out.
```

## Trust/compliance copy

Use:

```text
Clinic identity included
STOP and HELP supported
No medical advice in automated texts
Office reviews patient replies
```

Expanded text:

```text
Messages should clearly identify the clinic.
Recipients can reply STOP to opt out and HELP for support.
Automated messages should not include diagnosis, treatment advice, or emergency guidance.
Your team stays responsible for reviewing replies and continuing the conversation.
```

Included list:


CTA:

```text
Start setup by email
```

## Forbidden or risky phrases

Do not use:

```text
HIPAA Compliant
guaranteed compliant
guaranteed appointments
AI-powered growth platform
urgent
limited time
diagnosis
treatment recommendation
prescription
emergency help
```

Avoid these CTAs unless Vlad explicitly asks:

```text
Start free trial
Book demo
Get started
Create account
Register
```

Preferred CTAs:

```text
View pricing
How it works
Start setup by email
Email support
```

## Copy quality checklist

Before finishing, check:

- no fake claims
- no repeated filler
- no vague SaaS buzzwords
- no unsupported compliance language
- no medical advice
- no spammy SMS language
- no mismatch between CTA and actual available functionality
