# Recovery Workflow for Website

This file explains the product recovery process for dental clinic owners and office managers.

Use this as human-facing website logic, not backend documentation.

## Short version

A patient calls the dental office, but the front desk misses the call.

Missed Calls Dental sends a polite SMS from the clinic.

The patient can reply.

The front desk reviews the reply and follows up.

## Website-friendly workflow

Use this simple flow on the website:

```text
Missed call → polite SMS → patient reply → front desk follow-up
```

Or:

```text
Patient calls → office misses it → patient gets a text → patient replies → office continues the conversation
```

## SMS example

Use a short, professional example:

```text
Hi, this is Bright Smile Dental. We missed your call. Would you like help scheduling an appointment?
```

Alternative:

```text
Thanks for reaching out to Bright Smile Dental. We missed your call, but we can still help. Reply here and our team will follow up.
```

## Patient reply examples

Show only a few examples. Do not overload the website.

Good examples:

```text
Yes, I need an appointment.
Please call me back.
I have a question.
Wrong number.
STOP
```

## What happens next

Explain it simply:

- If the patient wants an appointment, the front desk follows up.
- If the patient asks for a call back, the office can call them.
- If the patient has a question, the team reviews it and responds.
- If the patient says wrong number, the office can close it out.
- If the patient replies STOP, they opt out of SMS.

## Important framing

The product does not replace the front desk.

It brings missed callers back into a conversation so the office can respond.

The system should not provide medical advice, diagnosis, treatment recommendations, prescriptions, or emergency guidance.

## How to use this on the website

Do not show this as a complex decision tree.

Use it as:

- a simple hero workflow visual
- a short “How it works” section
- a realistic SMS example
- a small “What patients can reply” section
- a front-desk follow-up explanation

Keep it short, clear, and owner-friendly.
