# Website Guidelines

## Product Positioning
- Product name: Dental Missed Call Recovery
- Positioning line: Dental Missed Call Recovery
- Primary job-to-be-done: Help dental offices recover missed inbound calls by sending an automatic, professional SMS follow-up.
- Core narrative: Missed calls can become lost appointment opportunities; immediate SMS follow-up helps offices reconnect.

## Target Audience
- Primary:
  - Dental clinic owners
  - Office managers
  - Front desk decision makers
- Secondary:
  - Operations leads evaluating communication workflows
- Buyer mindset:
  - Practical, risk-aware, time-constrained
  - Wants a clear ROI path without hype

## Core Promise
- When an office misses a call, the product automatically sends a professional SMS so staff can follow up and recover appointment opportunities.

## What The Website Is
- A focused B2B SaaS marketing site for one use case: missed-call SMS recovery for dental offices.
- A credibility-first conversion path to demo/trial/contact.

## What The Website Is Not
- Not a patient-facing dental clinic site.
- Not a general dental marketing agency site.
- Not a broad automation platform.
- Not an AI-everything product narrative.

## Required Tone
- Clear
- Direct
- Professional
- Trustworthy
- Specific
- Low-hype

## Required Page Structure
- Homepage must include:
  - Clear problem statement
  - Simple workflow (missed call -> SMS -> reply -> follow-up)
  - SMS example
  - Trust/compliance signals
  - Pricing or demo path
  - FAQ
- Required policy pages linked in global nav/footer:
  - Privacy Policy
  - Terms
  - SMS Consent/Program details
  - Contact

## Copywriting Rules
- Use concrete language tied to missed-call recovery only.
- Keep headlines short and literal.
- Emphasize office workflow outcomes, not vague transformation claims.
- Use realistic wording around opportunity recovery; avoid guaranteed results.
- Keep SMS examples professional and consent-aware.
- Always identify sender clearly in sample messaging.

## UX Rules
- Design for quick scanning by busy operators.
- Keep hierarchy obvious: problem -> solution -> proof of fit -> CTA.
- Limit cognitive load: short sections, clear labels, predictable layout.
- Every section must answer either "What is this?", "How does it work?", or "Can I trust this?"
- Mobile-first: CTA and key value proposition visible without excessive scrolling.

## Compliance Rules (Non-Legal Guidance)
- Include clear SMS opt-out behavior (STOP) and support behavior (HELP) in relevant copy.
- Include "message and data rates may apply" where SMS program terms are described.
- Avoid medical claims, diagnosis language, or treatment promises.
- Maintain truthful product description and sender identity.
- Avoid deceptive urgency, dark patterns, or misleading guarantees.

## Stripe / Twilio Readiness Notes
- Stripe readiness expectations:
  - Clear product description and who it serves
  - Visible business identity and contact details
  - Clear pricing or demo-based sales path
  - Accessible Privacy and Terms pages
- Twilio readiness expectations:
  - Clear SMS use-case explanation
  - Opt-out/HELP behavior documented
  - Non-spammy message examples
  - Consistent sender identity and business legitimacy signals
- Do not claim approval/certification unless actually granted.

## Accessibility Requirements
- Meet WCAG-oriented baseline patterns:
  - Sufficient color contrast
  - Semantic headings in order
  - Keyboard-focus visible states
  - Descriptive link and button text
  - Form labels and error clarity
- Avoid text embedded in images for critical content.
- Ensure responsive readability at small viewport widths.

## Forbidden Design / Content Patterns
- No stock dental photos.
- No patient treatment imagery.
- No children, dentist chairs, smiles, teeth closeups, or generic clinic photos.
- No fake statistics.
- No fake testimonials.
- No exaggerated revenue promises.
- No medical promises.
- No spammy SMS language.
- No vague phrases such as:
  - "AI-powered growth platform"
  - "optimize patient journey"
  - "all-in-one dental automation"
- No scope creep beyond missed-call SMS recovery.
