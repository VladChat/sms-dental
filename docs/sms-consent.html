SMS program details

SMS Consent and Opt-Out

Dental Missed Call Recovery is an operational text messaging service operated by AllyExporter LLC for dental-office missed-call recovery. The service is designed for customer care and scheduling follow-up, not marketing blasts.

How a caller may receive SMS

A participating dental clinic may use this service when a person calls the clinic and the call is missed, busy, or unanswered. The clinic's phone system may forward the missed call to a recovery number, and the service may send an SMS asking whether the caller wants a callback or scheduling help by text.

Participating clinics are responsible for using the service only where they have appropriate permission or another valid basis to send operational SMS follow-up, and for following applicable laws and carrier rules.

Example first SMS

Hi, this is [Dental Office Name]. Sorry we missed your call. Would you like us to call you back, or can we help by text? Reply STOP to opt out.

Message purpose

  • Missed-call acknowledgement
  • Callback coordination
  • Scheduling request follow-up
  • Basic routing to the front desk
  • Urgency flagging for staff attention without diagnosis or treatment advice

Message frequency

Message frequency varies by interaction. A typical missed-call sequence may include one first-response SMS and limited follow-up messages if the caller does not reply. Automated follow-ups are limited and stop when the caller replies, calls back, opts out, or the clinic closes the opportunity.

Opt out

Recipients can opt out at any time by replying STOP. After STOP, the service should stop sending further automated SMS to that number unless the recipient opts back in where permitted.

Help

Recipients can reply HELP for support information. They may also contact AllyExporter LLC at office@allyexporter.com.

Rates and carrier notice

Message and data rates may apply. Carriers are not liable for delayed or undelivered messages.

No medical advice or emergency service

This SMS program does not provide diagnosis, treatment advice, prescriptions, emergency response, or clinical decision-making. For emergencies, call 911 or seek emergency care immediately.
