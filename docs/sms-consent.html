SMS consent and opt-out

Missed Calls Dental sends short, operational SMS follow-up after a missed dental office call. This page explains why someone may receive a text and how opt-out works.

Why someone may receive a text

A caller may receive an SMS when they call a participating dental office and the call is missed, busy, or unanswered. The message identifies the clinic and invites the caller to reply.

Opt-out and support

Recipients can reply STOP to opt out of future automated messages, or reply HELP for support information. Message and data rates may apply.

No medical advice

Automated SMS messages do not include diagnosis, treatment recommendations, prescriptions, or emergency guidance. Recipients should contact their clinic directly for clinical questions, or seek emergency services for medical emergencies.

Office review

Replies are reviewed by the office. Your clinic stays responsible for scheduling, clinical decisions, and continuing the conversation with patients.

Contact

For SMS or opt-out questions, email support@missedcallsdental.com.
