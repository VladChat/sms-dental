Simple setup

A recovery layer, not a phone replacement.

The clinic keeps its current phone number. Missed Call SMS works as a backup path for no-answer or busy calls.

1

Forward missed calls

The clinic configures no-answer or busy call forwarding from its existing phone provider to a recovery number.

2

Send a fast SMS

After a missed call, the caller receives a short operational SMS asking whether they want a callback or help by text.

3

Front desk follows up

The conversation is routed to the front desk so staff can call back, answer scheduling questions, and mark the outcome.

Dental-specific workflows

Built around the calls dental offices actually miss.

New patient calls

Recover missed new-patient inquiries before they call another clinic.

Cleanings and checkups

Let callers request a callback or continue with basic scheduling help.

Reschedule requests

Give existing patients a quick way to reconnect with the office.

Urgent tooth pain

Flag urgent replies for front-desk attention without giving diagnosis or treatment advice.

After-hours calls

Use clear after-hours messaging that sets expectations for the next business day.

Owner visibility

Track missed calls, replies, callbacks, booked opportunities, and lost opportunities.

Compliance-minded messaging

Short, operational, and careful with sensitive information.

The MVP is designed to avoid diagnosis, treatment advice, prescriptions, insurance collection, or detailed medical history over automated SMS.

Included in the SMS program

  • Missed-call acknowledgement
  • Callback coordination
  • Scheduling intent routing
  • STOP opt-out language
  • HELP support language
  • Message and data rates notice
Important: Missed Call SMS is not an emergency service, not a medical advice tool, and not a replacement for the dental office's clinical judgment. If someone has a medical emergency, they should call 911 or seek emergency care.
